Introduction

History is concerned with the construction of narratives from extant traces of the past. Inevitably this is a fragmentary and partial process. Some stories are told and some remain ‘hidden from history’. The history of Gertrud Bodenwiser as dancer, teacher and choreographer is largely reliant upon an embodied legacy which circulates in the dance practices of individuals who have come into contact with her work. Dance knowledge is largely an uembodied tradition — we learn to dance by observing the movement of others.
